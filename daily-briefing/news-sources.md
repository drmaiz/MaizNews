# Trusted News Sources Directory

## Tier 1 — Authoritative (Primary)
These sources have strong editorial standards, fact-checking, and broad coverage.

### General News
- **Reuters** — Global news, AP-style reporting, strong fact-checking
- **Associated Press (AP)** — Original reporting, neutral tone, reliable sourcing
- **BBC** — International perspective, balanced coverage
- **NPR** — Investigative journalism, in-depth analysis

### Business & Markets
- **Financial Times** — Financial markets, business analysis, premium journalism
- **Wall Street Journal** — Market data, business reporting, opinion section
- **Bloomberg** — Real-time financial data, global business news

### Technology
- **Ars Technica** — Tech deep-dives, policy analysis, consumer technology
- **MIT Technology Review** — AI/science focus, technical accuracy
- **The Verge** — Tech industry, consumer electronics, policy

## Tier 2 — Strong Editorial Standards
These sources are reputable with consistent quality but may have some editorial voice.

- **The Guardian** — Investigative journalism, global perspective
- **ProPublica** — Investigative journalism, public interest focus
- **The New York Times** — News reporting (separate from opinion)
- **The Washington Post** — Political reporting, investigative pieces

## Tier 3 — Specialist Sources
High-quality but narrower focus.

- **Nature** — Scientific research, peer-reviewed findings
- **Science Daily** — Science research summaries
- **Medical News Today** — Health & medical reporting
- **HackerNews** (aggregator) — Tech & startup community intelligence
- **Axios** — Succinct news format, tech & politics

## Sources to Avoid for Briefings
❌ Raw social media posts (X, Reddit, etc.) without verification
❌ Entertainment tabloids
❌ Explicitly partisan opinion outlets (use news sections only)
❌ Unverified blogs without editorial oversight
❌ Clickbait/sensationalist headlines

## How to Verify Source Quality
1. **Check editorial standards** — Does the outlet have a public ethics/standards page?
2. **Look for corrections** — Quality outlets publish corrections transparently
3. **Verify sourcing** — Are claims attributed to named sources or documents?
4. **Check author credentials** — Is the reporter a staff member or freelancer?
5. **Cross-reference** — Is the story covered by multiple quality outlets?

## Briefing Best Practices
1. **No more than 2 items from same source** per briefing
2. **Prioritize breaking/developing stories** over soft news
3. **Verify dates** — don't mix old and new stories
4. **Check for duplicates** — same story from different sources = pick best version
5. **Balance categories** — avoid all tech or all business
6. **Timestamp all items** — helps readers understand recency
